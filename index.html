Guide-AI Workshop @ SIGMOD2025

Guide-AI seeks to foster convergence between data management researchers and the broad Responsible AI researchers from neighboring communities like AI, Machine Learning, and Theoretical Computer Science. The workshop will publish both regular research papers (8 pages plus unlimited references) and short papers (up to 4 pages) addressing challenges, visionary ideas, and tools related to Responsible AI.

Keynote Speakers

Steven Whang

Title: Towards a Holistic Framework for Data-centric Responsible AI

Responsible AI is becoming critical as AI is widely used in our everyday lives. In this talk, Steven Whang overviews data-centric responsible AI research at KAIST, covering fairness techniques, robust training, privacy using disinformation, and explainability-first clustering.

Covered papers:

  • Roh et al., “FairBatch: Batch Selection for Model Fairness”, ICLR 2021
  • Zhang et al., “iFlipper: Label Flipping for Individual Fairness”, SIGMOD 2023
  • Tae et al., “Falcon: Fair Active Learning using Multi-armed Bandits”, VLDB 2024
  • Tae and Whang, “Slice Tuner: A Selective Data Acquisition Framework for Accurate and Fair ML Models”, SIGMOD 2021
  • Roh et al., “Sample Selection for Fair and Robust Training”, NeurIPS 2021
  • Heo and Whang, “Redactor: A Data-Centric Defense against Inference Attacks”, AAAI 2023
  • Hwang and Whang, “XClusters: Explainability-first Clustering”, AAAI 2023

Bio: Steven Euijong Whang is an associate professor at KAIST EE and AI, leading the Data Intelligence Lab. His research includes Responsible AI and Data-centric AI, and he has held positions at Google Research and served as an editor for various IEEE journals.

Felix Naumann

Title: Data Quality in the Age of AI

Felix Naumann discusses the evolving notion of data quality in the context of AI. With increasing reliance on data-driven methods and recent regulations like the EU AI Act, data quality now encompasses aspects such as fairness, diversity, and explainability.

Bio: Felix Naumann studied at the University of Technology in Berlin and completed his PhD at Humboldt University. He is a professor at HPI, University of Potsdam, and has published over 200 scientific papers on data profiling, data quality, and data integration.

Panel Discussion

Session: The Role of Data Management Research for Responsible AI

Panelists: Steven Whang, Felix Naumann, Boris Glavic, Fatemeh Nargesian, Leopoldo Bertossi, Ziawasch Abedjan

In this panel, leading experts from both the data management and responsible AI communities discuss the challenges and opportunities for convergence. Topics include integrating data-centric methods with AI model development, identifying data challenges that hinder responsible AI, and exploring innovative solutions to foster cross-community collaboration.
